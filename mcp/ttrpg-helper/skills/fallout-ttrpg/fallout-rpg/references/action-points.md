# Action Points - full economy

Load this when AP economy questions come up, when the group pool is empty and a player wants to "fund the GM" to buy d20s, or when combat AP spends are needed.

## Generation

- Every success above the difficulty on a successful test becomes 1 AP.
- AP go into a single **group pool** shared by all players. Anyone can spend from it.
- The group pool **caps at 6 AP**. AP that would push the pool past 6 are lost.

## All ways to spend AP

### Spends usable in any scene

| Cost | Effect |
|:---:|---|
| 1 / 2 / 3 AP | **Buy d20s** for a test - 1 AP for the first bonus die, 2 for the second, 3 for the third. Maximum +3 d20s (so a max pool of 5d20). Bought before rolling, after the GM has set the difficulty. |
| 1 AP | **Obtain information** - ask the GM one question about the situation, related to the test you just passed. The GM answers truthfully but may answer incompletely. |
| 2 AP | **Reduce time** - halve the time the test takes to attempt (relevant when time pressure matters in fiction). |

### Spends usable only in combat

| Cost | Effect |
|:---:|---|
| 1 AP | **Take an additional minor action** on your turn. Cap of 2 minor actions per round total. |
| 2 AP | **Take an additional major action** on your turn. Any skill test required by the extra major action has its difficulty increased by +1. Cap of 2 major actions per round total. |
| 1-3 AP | **Add extra damage** on a successful melee or thrown-weapon attack: +1 CD per AP spent, max +3 CD. |

## Bonus d20 costs (reference table)

| Pool size | Bonus d20s | Cumulative AP cost |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 2d20 | - | 0 |
| 3d20 | +1 | 1 |
| 4d20 | +2 | 3 |
| 5d20 | +3 | 6 |

Spent AP costs are cumulative within a single test - buying the third die means paying 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 AP.

## Timing - when AP is spent

- **Buying d20s**: spent *before* rolling, *after* the GM sets difficulty. You cannot top up after you have already rolled.
- **Obtain information / reduce time / damage / extra action**: spent *after* the relevant roll resolves.

## Saving AP

- AP not spent immediately stay in the group pool, capped at 6.
- The pool persists across scenes and sessions - it is not reset between encounters.

## The GM's pool

- Each session, the GM starts with **1 AP per player** in their own pool.
- The GM's pool has **no cap**.
- The GM spends from their pool to fuel NPC actions: buying d20s for NPC tests, taking extra actions, etc.

## How the GM gains AP

- **Player rolls a complication** that the table cannot find a fictional consequence for: the GM may take 1 AP instead of imposing the complication.
- **Player loses an opposed test** the GM rolled first: the GM gains 1 AP per success the player fell short by.
- **Player "buys d20s without AP"** (see below): every die the player buys this way puts 1 AP in the GM's pool.

## Buying d20s without AP - "fund the GM"

When the group pool is empty and a player still wants extra d20s on a test, they can fund the GM instead. Per AP they want to spend on bonus d20s, the GM gains 1 AP. The cost ladder is the same (1 AP for +1 die, 2 for +2, 3 for +3).

This trade applies **only** to buying d20s. Players cannot pay the GM in this way for any other AP spend (no buying information, no reducing time, no extra actions).

## Two PCs opposed - special rule

When two player characters make an opposed test against each other:

- Neither side may spend from the **group pool** to buy d20s.
- Either or both sides may "fund the GM" instead - paying AP into the GM's pool to buy d20s for themselves.

This keeps the cooperative group pool out of intra-party conflicts.
