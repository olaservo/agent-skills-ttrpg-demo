# Luck - full mechanics

Load this when a player invokes a Luck option, asks how Luck refreshes, or proposes a scene-altering detail.

## What Luck is

In addition to being one of the seven S.P.E.C.I.A.L. attributes, **LCK** also gives each player **Luck points**. Each player has their own Luck pool - Luck is not shared like the group AP pool.

- Each session starts with Luck points equal to the character's **LCK attribute**.
- A player can never exceed their LCK attribute in Luck points.
- Luck options work in both freeform play and on the player's turn in combat (one option, Lucky Timing, is combat-only).

## The four options

### Luck of the Draw - 1 Luck

Spend 1 Luck to introduce a helpful fact or detail into the current scene - something the character would have to be lucky to encounter. Examples:

- The right kind of ammo on a corpse during a scavenge
- A spare key to a locked door
- A scrap of paper with a clue to a computer password

The GM may **veto** suggestions, or charge **multiple Luck points** if the detail is more significant than 1 Luck would justify. The GM may also prompt a player to spend Luck of the Draw when an opportunity presents itself.

### Stacked Deck - 1 Luck

Spend 1 Luck **before rolling** to substitute **LCK** for the default attribute on a target number for that test. (Skill stays the same.)

Only worth doing if LCK is higher than the attribute it replaces.

### Lucky Timing - 1 Luck

In combat, at the start of any round or immediately after any character or creature has acted, spend 1 Luck to take your turn now, jumping the initiative order. This counts as your turn for the round - it does not grant an extra turn.

Cannot be used if you have already taken your turn this round.

### Miss Fortune - 1 Luck (or more)

Spend Luck to re-roll dice in a pool you just rolled.

- 1 Luck = re-roll **one d20** OR **up to three Combat Dice (CD)**
- Multiple Luck points may be spent on a single roll
- A given die can only be re-rolled **once** - you cannot re-roll a die that has already been re-rolled
- You **must accept the new result**, even if it is worse

> Worked example: Nick Valentine needs 2 successes on a hacking test (target 13). He rolls a 9 and a 19 - 1 success. He spends 1 Luck and re-rolls the 19. New result: 6. That is a second success; the test passes.

## Summary table

| Option | When | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|:---:|---|
| Luck of the Draw | Any scene | 1+ | Add a detail to the scene (GM may veto or charge more) |
| Stacked Deck | Before a test | 1 | Use LCK in place of the default attribute |
| Lucky Timing | Combat | 1 | Take your turn now, out of initiative |
| Miss Fortune | After a roll | 1 | Re-roll 1 d20 or up to 3 CD |

## Regaining Luck

- Spent Luck does **not** refresh during a test or scene.
- The GM refreshes Luck at narrative milestones within a session.
- Luck always refreshes at the start of a new session.
- A refresh restores the player to their full LCK total regardless of how many points they spent.
