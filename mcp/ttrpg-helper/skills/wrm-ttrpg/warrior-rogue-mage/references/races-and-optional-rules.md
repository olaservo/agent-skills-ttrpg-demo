# Non-Human Races & Optional Rules

These are **optional** rules. By default WR&M characters are human; the GM decides whether
non-human races are available in their setting.

## Non-human player characters

At the start of character creation a player may choose a race from the list below (or the GM may
create more). The character **automatically receives all talents listed as racial talents** for
that race - these are **in addition to** the one talent every character picks. Some racial
talents are drawbacks, included to balance the advantages.

| Race | Description | Racial talents |
|---|---|---|
| **Elf** | Slender, beautiful, pointed-eared, long-lived; exceptional magical talent. | Exceptional Attribute (Mage), Sixth Sense, Weak |
| **Dwarf** | Shorter, stockier, bearded; very strong and tough for their size; renowned fighters. | Exceptional Attribute (Warrior), Craftsman (craft of choice), No Talent for Magic |
| **Halfling** | Even shorter than dwarves, often mistaken for human children; peaceful, but shine at thievery when adventuring. | Exceptional Attribute (Rogue), Hunter, Weak |
| **Lizardman** | Intelligent lizard-evolved humanoids; warm-blooded but prefer hot, dry areas; scaled; known for lack of strong emotion and keen logic. | Natural Armor (Defense 2), Tough as Nails, Outcast |
| **Goblin** | Smallest of the greenskins (~3 ft), green-skinned, long-eared, razor teeth; a knack for salvaging and tinkering. | Tinkerer, Lucky Devil, Weak |
| **Orc** | Slightly larger than humans, green-skinned, exceptionally strong; savage warriors with a strong sense of honor; often accompanied by tame black wolves. | Berserker, Exceptional Attribute (Warrior), Outcast |

With GM approval, players of other races may pick some of these talents too, and the GM is
encouraged to invent new racial talents.

## Racial talents

- **Berserker** - Go berserk during combat: **+2 Warrior** and **+2 to all damage caused**. A
  berserk character temporarily ignores all damage effects: note the total damage taken and
  apply it when the rage ends. **Mage is reduced to 0** during the rage (no casting). You must
  attack the nearest enemy and may only fight in close combat (melee or unarmed). When all
  enemies are dead/incapacitated, make an **Easy (DL 5)** check using normal (unmodified) Mage;
  on success the rage ends and the character drops unconscious for 2d6 minutes (waking with a
  headache); a character reduced to 0 or fewer HP is dead or dying. On a failed check the
  berserker must attack the nearest **ally** but keeps rolling each turn to come out of berserk.
- **Exceptional Attribute** - When making a check with the chosen attribute, roll **two d6 and
  keep the highest**.
- **Natural Armor** - Scales or thick fur grant Defense (e.g. Defense 2). Works only as long as
  **no other armor** is worn.
- **No Talent for Magic** - On a casting check, roll **two d6 and keep the lowest**; all base
  mana costs are **doubled**. (Armor Penalty is unaffected.)
- **Weak** - Start play with HP equal to **3 + Warrior**, and on advancement gain only **1d6-2
  HP (minimum 1)**.
- **Outcast** - Considered an outcast in most societies: every social-interaction test with a
  member of a different race is modified by **-3**.
- **Tinkerer** - **+2** on all checks for repairing, dismantling, or using technical equipment
  (mechanical traps, firearms, war golems, clockwork, etc.).
- *(Sixth Sense, Hunter, Lucky Devil, Craftsman are the same as the general talents in
  `skills-and-talents.md`.)*

## Dual-wielding and multiple attacks

The **Dual Wielding** talent lets a character wield two weapons but does **not** grant an extra
attack. If the GM approves, a dual-wielder may take the **Double Attack** talent (which requires
Dual Wielding). With Double Attack, the character gets an **extra attack with the off-hand
weapon** in a round. Both weapons must be the **same type** - two melee or two ranged (e.g. twin
swords, dragon pistols, or sword-and-dagger), but **not** one ranged and one melee.
**Two-handed weapons cannot be dual-wielded.**

## Mounted and vehicle combat

Mounted and vehicle combat work much like normal combat, with the addition of **Maneuver
checks**. A character performing a maneuver on a mount or moving vehicle makes a Maneuver check
at a DL set by the GM - a **Riding** check on a mount, a **Driving** check in a vehicle. Just
moving in a straight line is **not** a maneuver (as long as the rider/driver is not fighting).

| Maneuver | DL |
|---|:--:|
| Walk (horse) | - |
| Canter (horse) | 5 |
| Gallop (horse) | 9 |
| Tight turn | 7 |
| Sudden stop | 9 |
| Jump obstacle | 7 |

On a successful maneuver check the character may also perform combat actions (attacks, reloading,
etc.). On a failure they may fall from the horse or crash; if the maneuver was riding/driving in
a straight line, they must reduce speed immediately.
