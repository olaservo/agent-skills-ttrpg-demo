# Magic Items

These are the **six sample** magic items from the rulebook. GMs are encouraged to invent their
own or modify these to create more exotic treasure.

| Item | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Healing Potion** | A vial of red bubbling liquid that heals **1d6 HP** when quaffed. |
| **Mana Potion** | A vial of blue bubbling potion that restores **1d6 Mana** when quaffed. |
| **Gauntlets of Titanic Strength** | Large gauntlets that cause **2d6 damage** when used for unarmed attacks. |
| **Feathered Cloak** | A cloak covered in raven feathers that slows every fall, so the wearer takes **no fall damage**. |
| **Warmage Armor** | Plate armor from the time of the Imperium, enchanted so casters can wear it without penalty. Its statistics equal normal plate armor, but its **Armor Penalty is 0**. |
| **Runeblade** | A rune-covered sword that **ignores any armor**: attack rolls with it are made against the target's **basic (unarmored) Defense**. |

## Designing new magic items

Magic items in WR&M are GM fiat - there is no crafting subsystem. To make a new item, anchor its
effect to an existing rule: a flat bonus to attack/damage/Defense, a stored spell (see magic
implements in `magic.md`), a per-day charge of a known spell, a damage type that bypasses armor
(like the Runeblade), or a situational immunity (like the Feathered Cloak). Keep numbers in line
with the samples above so a single item does not eclipse a whole character build.
