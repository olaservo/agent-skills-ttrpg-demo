# Factions, Powers & Hooks

The source sketches a handful of factions and then explicitly invites the GM to extend them. What
follows separates **what the book states** from **suggested hooks** you can keep or discard.

## Factions and powers (from the source)

- **The Imperial Faith** — the major religion of the continent, led by the **Patriarch** based in
  Chaetril. Its militant arm is the **Paladins**, an elite unit of church knights housed in
  Chaetril's cathedral. (A Priest of the Faith typically has the *Champion (Imperial Faith)*
  talent - see the bestiary in `warrior-rogue-mage`.)
- **The Falcon Knights of Vaikus** — an elite military unit and one of the few that still carry
  the antique **dragon pistols** as sidearms; symbols of Vaikus's relative wealth and order.
- **The Scorpion Guard of Joakalavi** — the only body permitted to bear arms inside the neutral
  trade city of Joakalavi; effectively the law in the continent's commercial heart.
- **The warlords** — a few claim the empty Imperial throne (Cemimus's ruling line descends from
  Aurelius III himself), but none can reunite the city-states. The throne is a prize, not a fact.
- **The Dark Spire (Traevar)** — the last surviving magical academy of Imperial times; the
  setting's natural home for mages, lost spells, and arcane intrigue.
- **Golems and lost Imperial tech** — work golems once ran the economy; war golems guarded the
  borders before being destroyed or outlawed. Surviving constructs, moongates, and dragon
  firearms are the relics a campaign is built on. (Golem and construct stats are in the
  `warrior-rogue-mage` bestiary.)

## Open questions (the book leaves these blank — answer them for your table)

The source explicitly poses these as seeds:

- **Who rules the city of Bekel** — and can it survive Cemimus's war?
- **What is the Imperial Faith actually like** — benevolent, corrupt, fractured?
- **What is going on in Tukrael** — what raised its dead, and what guards the ruins?

The book also encourages you to bend the setting freely: *don't like that the Falcon Knights
carry pistols? Change it. Want invading barbarian hordes from the east, or orcs? Put them in.
It's your sandbox.*

## Suggested adventure hooks

Original prompts in the spirit of the setting (use, modify, or ignore):

- **Tomb-robbing in Tukrael.** A Bekelan guild hires the party to recover an Imperial relic from
  the dead capital before Cemimus's agents do - with the undead in between.
- **The siege of Bekel.** Cemimus's mercenaries close on the mining city; the party can break the
  siege, smuggle out the master smiths, or sabotage the tunnels.
- **A pistol for a Falcon Knight.** A disgraced Vaikusian knight needs a working dragon pistol -
  and the only one left is in a rival caste's vault, or a desert ruin near Joakalavi.
- **Caravan to Joakalavi.** Escort a caravan across the Central Desert to the neutral trade city,
  where the Scorpion Guard's "no arms" law turns a simple deal into a powder keg.
- **The Spire's missing student.** A mage of the Dark Spire has vanished with a forbidden ritual;
  Traevar's masters want them found before a moongate is misused.
- **The Patriarch's request.** The Imperial Faith dispatches the party (perhaps with a Paladin
  escort) to investigate a "miracle" in the Chaetril grasslands that smells of old Imperial
  technology.

Tie any hook back to a stat block, spell, or hazard in `warrior-rogue-mage` so it is ready to
run.
