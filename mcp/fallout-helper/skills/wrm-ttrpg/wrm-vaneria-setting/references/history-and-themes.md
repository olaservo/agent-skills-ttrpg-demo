# History & Themes

## The rise and fall of the Imperium

In ages past the **Vanerian people** conquered the whole continent and built what the world
remembers as the most glorious civilization in human history. Vanerian citizens lived in peace
and prosperity, lifted by extraordinary **technological and magical advancement**:

- **Golems** did most of the hard and dangerous labor.
- **War golems** defended the Imperium's borders.
- Wonders such as the **dragon firearms** (the dragon pistol and rifle) were invented during this
  golden age, and **moongates** linked distant places for instant travel.

That peace ended when the last Emperor, **Aurelius III**, died. His would-be successors started a
**bloody civil war**, and the Imperium tore itself apart.

## Five hundred years later

By the "present day" of the setting, roughly **five centuries** have passed since the war, and
little of the glory remains:

- The continent is a patchwork of feuding **city-states**, successors of the old Imperium.
- A few **warlords** still claim the Imperial throne, but **none has the power to reunite** the
  warring states.
- **Most war golems were destroyed** in the war; the survivors were **outlawed centuries ago**,
  though rumors and rogue constructs persist.
- The former capital is a haunted ruin (see `gazetteer.md`), and lost Imperial technology and
  magic lie scattered in ruins across the land.

## Tone and themes

The source frames WR&M as **epic fantasy** built on a foundation of decline and rediscovery. The
emotional engine of a Vaneria campaign is the gap between a magnificent past and a fractured
present:

- **Ruins and lost lore.** Powerful artifacts, forgotten technologies, and ruined cities are
  exactly what players expect when they sit down - so the GM should deliver them.
- **A power vacuum.** No single authority rules; ambitious warlords, scheming city-states, and a
  powerful church compete to fill the void.
- **Sandbox by design.** The setting is openly incomplete. The book's own advice: *start slow so
  players learn the lethal combat, then turn it up to epic.* Add lands, monsters, and factions
  freely - this is a seed, not a straitjacket.

> GM note: the history above is the entire "canon." Everything else - the nature of the Imperial
> Faith, who really rules Bekel, what stirs in Tukrael - is deliberately left open for you to
> decide. See the open questions in `factions-and-hooks.md`.
