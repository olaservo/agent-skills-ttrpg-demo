# Character Creation

A WR&M character is built in seven short steps. There are **no classes** - a character is
defined by how their attribute points are split, the three skills they start with, and one
talent.

## The seven steps

1. **Distribute 10 attribute levels** among the three attributes - **Warrior**, **Rogue**, and
   **Mage**. No attribute may start higher than **6**. An attribute may be left at 0, but a
   Mage of 0 cannot cast even the simplest spell, and you may not take a skill tied to an
   attribute of 0.
2. **Pick three skills.** Skills are not ranked - you either know a skill or you do not. Each
   skill is tied to one attribute (see `skills-and-talents.md`); a known, applicable skill adds
   **+2** to a check and lets the die explode on a 6.
3. **Pick one talent.** Talents grant special abilities that set a PC apart from common folk.
   See `skills-and-talents.md`. (Some talents may be taken more than once.)
4. **(Optional) Choose a non-human race.** If you do, record the race's **racial talents** on
   your sheet for free (these are in addition to your one chosen talent). See
   `races-and-optional-rules.md`.
5. **Calculate derived stats:**
   - **Hit Points (HP) = 6 + Warrior**
   - **Fate = Rogue** (you still start with **1** Fate even if Rogue is 0)
   - **Mana = 2 × Mage**
   - **Defense = ⌊(Warrior + Rogue) / 2⌋ + 4** (round the half down)
6. **Purchase starting equipment with 250 silver pieces (SP).** Every character can wield every
   weapon. Spellcasters may wear armor, but the armor's Armor Penalty (AP) raises the mana cost
   of every spell. See `equipment.md`.
7. **Fill out the character sheet** with all values, weapons, armor, talents, items, and any
   starting spells.

## Notes and guidance

- A **well-rounded** character with a few levels in each attribute works best for new players;
  highly specialized builds (e.g. Warrior 6 / Rogue 4 / Mage 0) are possible but fragile in
  their weak areas.
- A high **Warrior** is a born fighter: strong, tough, fearless. A high **Rogue** is witty,
  stealthy, and quick of hand and mind. A high **Mage** signals intelligence, willpower, and
  mental discipline. Low scores mean the opposite (clumsy, dull, easily influenced).
- **Spells are not free at creation.** A starting caster must *buy* spells (25/50/75/100 SP for
  1st/2nd/3rd/4th circle) out of the 250 SP, transcribe them into a spell book (20 SP
  leatherbound), and may want a magic implement. Plan the budget accordingly. See `magic.md`
  and `equipment.md`.
- Characters do **not** gain XP or levels. They improve through GM-granted **advancement** after
  adventures - see `game-mastering.md`.

## Quick example

*Brann, a human warrior.* Attributes Warrior 5 / Rogue 3 / Mage 2 (= 10). Skills: Swords, Axes,
Athletics. Talent: Massive Attack. Derived: HP = 6 + 5 = **11**; Fate = **3**; Mana = 2 × 2 =
**4**; Defense = ⌊(5 + 3)/2⌋ + 4 = 4 + 4 = **8**. Spends 250 SP on a sword (5), leather armor
(15), an adventurer's kit (5), and travel gear, banking the rest.
